# Rector of Vilnius University Prof. Rimvydas Petrauskas

Dear Students,

I would like to sincerely thank you and express my appreciation for your active involvement both at the University and beyond. The Vilnius University Students' Representation is one of the most active, creative, and significant organizations in our community, uniting and representing students while also working more broadly for the benefit of an open University and an open Lithuania.

I'm thankful for your contribution to the development of the new VU strategy. Your voice is heard in the University’s strategic and day-to-day decisions, as well as in the life of the country. You are ones who want to be seen and heard, and you have meaningful ideas and constructive criticism that we take to heart as we continue to shape our shared future.

This isn't the first year that we have been working together constructively to make the University an even better place for study, research, and work, and an even stronger community.

I would like to emphasize that we stand out from other universities in the country not only for the quality and diversity of our academic programs and research, but also for our students’ active engagement and civic-mindedness. You make a significant contribution to our strategic goal of influencing public life and strengthening the ideals of civic democracy.

By working together, we can achieve even more in bringing about positive changes in the education system, improving the study environment and quality, and thus creating all the necessary conditions that would allow the younger generation to stay and continue their career and life paths in Lithuania.

One of the most notable aspects of VU SR’s work is that you act not only for your own generation but also for future generations of students. The work carried out this year lays the foundation for the coming years and shapes the guidelines for student policy for decades to come.

I always take an interest in and support your work, and I wish you continued determination and enthusiasm to reach for the stars.

## Rector of Vilnius University

## Prof. Rimvydas Petrauskas

## Akademinės etikos ir procedūrų kontrolierė Dr. Reda Cimmperman

Mieli Vilniaus universiteto studentai, mieli VU Studentų atstovybės nariai,

man visada gera matyti studentus, kurie ne tik dalyvauja universiteto gyvenime, bet ir jį kuria. Jūsų veikla rodo, kad studentų balsas gali būti labai stiprus – ne tik keliant klausimus, bet ir ieškant sprendimų, telkiant bendruomenę, primenant, kad universitetas yra gyvas tiek, kiek jame yra dialogo, pasitikėjimo ir atsakomybės.

Šiais metais mūsų keliai ne kartą susitiko kalbantis apie akademinę etiką. Ir man labai svarbu pasakyti – Jūsų įsitraukimas buvo tikras, nuoširdus ir matomas. Džiaugiuosi, kad šios temos neliko tik diskusijų lygmenyje, bet virto konkrečiais žingsniais, tarp jų ir atskiru veiksmų planu Vilniaus universitete. Tai rodo, kad pokytis prasideda tada, kai žmonės tiki, jog jų balsas turi prasmę.

Akademinė etika nėra vien taisyklės, dokumentai ar procedūros. Ji prasideda nuo kasdienio pasirinkimo – būti sąžiningiems, gerbti vieni kitus, kalbėtis, prisiimti atsakomybę ir nebijoti kelti nepatogių klausimų. Jūs parodote, kad studentai gali būti labai svarbi šios kultūros dalis.

Linkiu Jums ir toliau išlikti drąsiems, gyviems ir bendruomeniškiems. Linkiu neprarasti smalsumo, jautrumo ir tikėjimo, kad net maži žingsniai gali keisti universiteto kultūrą. O kiekvienam iš Jūsų – patirties, kuri augina, žmonių, kurie įkvepia, ir drąsos veikti taip, kaip diktuoja vertybės.

## Dr. Reda Cimmperman

## Akademinės etikos ir procedūrų kontrolierė

Dear students of Vilnius University, dear members of the Vilnius University Students’ Representation,

It is always inspiring to see students who not only take part in university life, but also help shape it. Your work shows that the student voice can be truly powerful – not only in raising questions, but also in looking for solutions, bringing people together and reminding us that a university is alive when there is dialogue, trust and responsibility.

This year, our paths crossed several times in discussions on academic ethics. I would like to say sincerely that your engagement has been real, thoughtful and visible. I am very glad that these conversations did not remain only at the level of ideas, but became concrete steps, including a separate action plan on academic ethics at Vilnius University. This shows that change begins when people believe that their voice matters.

Academic ethics is not only about rules, documents or procedures. It begins with everyday choices – to act honestly, to respect one another, to speak openly, to take responsibility and not to be afraid of asking difficult questions. You have shown that students are an essential part of building this culture.

I wish for you to remain brave, open, and community-minded. May you never lose your curiosity, sensitivity and belief that even small steps can change the culture of a university. And to each of you, I wish experiences that help you grow, people who inspire you and the courage to act according to your values.

## Dr. Reda Cimmperman

## Ombudswoman for Academic Ethics and Procedures

Director of The Centre for Quality Assessment in Higher Education Zinaida Manžuch

The Centre for Quality Assessment in Higher Education warmly congratulates the Vilnius University Students’ Representation on another active year of work.
